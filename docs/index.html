About This Project


The main objective of this project is to analyze air pollution in the Lombardy region of Italy, specifically Milan, create air quality index maps, and assess population exposure to different levels of pollution. The results will be presented through a website featuring WebGIS content. This goal will be achieved by leveraging air quality monitoring station data, meteorological data, traffic flow data, and geographic information on population distribution.

About the Study Area

Milan is the capital of the Lombardy region and the largest city in northern Italy, as well as one of Europe's major industrial and commercial centers. Milan is located on the Po Valley, surrounded by the Alps, a geographical position that limits air circulation and facilitates the accumulation of pollutants.

Air pollution in the Milan area mainly comes from traffic emissions, industrial activities, residential heating systems, and surrounding agricultural activities. In winter, due to temperature inversion phenomena, pollutants are often trapped above the city, leading to significant air quality deterioration. Additionally, Milan's high population density and level of urbanization exacerbate air pollution problems.

Air Quality Monitoring

Comprehensive monitoring of air pollutants including particulate matter (PM2.5, PM10), nitrogen dioxide (NO2), ozone (O3), and other key indicators using data from monitoring stations across the Milan metropolitan area.

Factor Analysis

Investigation of factors influencing air quality, including traffic patterns, land use, industrial activities, meteorological conditions, and seasonal variations to identify pollution sources and patterns.

Health Risk Assessment

Evaluation of potential health impacts on the population, focusing on vulnerable groups such as children, the elderly, and those with pre-existing respiratory conditions in areas with high pollution levels.

Project Results

Pollution Mapping

High-resolution maps of air pollutant concentrations across the Milan metropolitan area, showing spatial patterns and identifying hotspots of air pollution that require immediate attention.

Land Use Impact

Analysis of the relationship between land use types and air pollution levels, providing insights into how urban planning and land management affect air quality in different areas.

Temporal Analysis

Examination of air quality variations over time, including daily, seasonal, and annual patterns, helping to identify critical periods when pollution levels exceed health standards.

Interactive WebGIS

An interactive web-based geographic information system that allows users to explore air quality data, toggle between different pollutants, and view related geographic features in the study area.
