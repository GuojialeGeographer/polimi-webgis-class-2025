About the Project

The main objective of this project is to analyze air pollution across Greece, create air quality index maps, and assess population exposure to different levels of pollution. The results are presented through this website featuring WebGIS content. This goal is achieved by leveraging air quality monitoring station data, meteorological data, traffic flow data, and geographic information on population distribution.

About the Study Area

Greece is located in southeastern Europe with a diverse geography comprising mountains, peninsulas, and approximately 2,000 islands. With a Mediterranean climate characterized by hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters, Greece faces unique air quality challenges. The country's topography, with numerous mountain ranges dividing the mainland, creates distinctive microclimates and pollution dispersion patterns.

Air pollution in Greece stems from multiple sources including urban traffic, shipping emissions around major ports, industrial activities, agricultural burning, and seasonal wildfires. During summer, tourist influx significantly increases in coastal areas and islands, creating temporary pollution hotspots. Winter brings additional challenges as wood burning for home heating increases particulate matter concentrations, especially in urban areas and mountain villages. The complex interactions between these factors and Greece's varied topography make air quality management a significant challenge across the nation.
